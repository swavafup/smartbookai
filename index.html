Profile

Muhammed Swavaf is a Research Assistant and Data Scientist at the United Arab Emirates University, working across artificial intelligence, computer vision, deep learning, robotics, geospatial intelligence, medical imaging, and autonomous systems. His research focuses on intelligent perception systems, UAV/drone imagery, road defect detection, semantic segmentation, vision transformers, driver distraction analysis, humanoid agents, GPU-accelerated deep learning, and reproducible AI research.

Geospatial AI & Smart Mobility

Vision Transformers

Medical Imaging AI

Human-Robot Interaction

Applied Deep Learning Systems

Selected Projects

Research Applications Portal

Developed a comprehensive CIT research website using Microsoft SharePoint, Power Apps, and Power BI, including request management, role-based access control, research lab pages, and graduate studies information.

HPC Applications

Leveraged NVIDIA DGX-1 and A100 systems to optimize training and deployment of deep learning models, and designed a user-friendly interface for researchers and students to access GPU resources.

Crack Detection

Collaborated with the Emirates Center for Mobility Research to develop vision transformer-based models for detecting and geolocating surface cracks in stone floor tiles and pavements using high-resolution geospatial imagery.

Driver Distraction Analysis

Developed simulator-based systems to study driver responses to text, image, alarm, and voice command reminders, contributing insights into human-vehicle interaction in autonomous vehicles.

Medical Imaging

Conducted computer vision research for brain tumor classification and segmentation using deep learning methodologies in association with data science and analytics teams.

Humanoid Agents

Partnered with Western Sydney University to study emotional perception and responses to auditory changes in humanoid agents using machine learning and behavioral analysis.
